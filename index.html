Innovative Spectrophotometer & Temperature Sensor

Quantum Vision is a multifunctional scientific device that performs concentration measurement via spectrophotometry and temperature sensing in one affordable, sustainable package.

How It Works

Concentration Measurement

Uses the Beer-Lambert Law to determine solution concentration by measuring light absorption through a sample.

A = εlc

Where A = absorbance, ε = molar absorptivity, l = path length, c = concentration.

Temperature Sensing

Utilizes an AMT18 temperature sensor with 99.9% accuracy to measure solution temperature based on thermal equilibrium principles.

q = -k(dT/dx)

Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction governs the heat transfer from solution to sensor.

AI Integration

Includes an AI assistant (QCHEM AXIS) to help students understand chemical concepts, troubleshoot experiments, and analyze results.

Trained on chemistry curriculum and experimental data to provide contextual, educational support.

Sensor Placement

Optimal positioning of LDR and temperature sensor relative to light source and sample ensures maximum measurement accuracy and repeatability.

Scientific Principles & Theory

Beer-Lambert Law Fundamentals

Theoretical Basis

The Beer-Lambert Law describes the linear relationship between absorbance and concentration of an absorbing species:

A = εlc

Where:

  • A = Absorbance (unitless)
  • ε = Molar absorptivity (L·mol⁻¹·cm⁻¹)
  • l = Path length through sample (cm)
  • c = Concentration (mol·L⁻¹)

For potassium permanganate at 525 nm: ε = 2200 L·mol⁻¹·cm⁻¹, l = 0.5 cm

Beer-Lambert Law Verification

Thermodynamics & Heat Transfer

Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics

Heat transfers between solution and sensor until thermal equilibrium is reached:

Tsensor = Tsolution

When sensor (26°C) is placed in solution (34°C): ΔT = 8°C

Fourier's Law of Heat Conduction

Describes heat flow from higher to lower temperature:

q = -k(dT/dx)

Where q = heat flux, k = thermal conductivity, dT/dx = temperature gradient.

Newton's Law of Cooling

Describes rate of temperature change:

dT/dt = -k(T - Ta)

For our system: dT/dt = -0.2(26 - 34) = 1.6°C/s

Electromagnetic Radiation & Photonics

Photon Energy Equation

E = hν = hc/λ

Where:

  • E = Energy of photon (J)
  • h = Planck's constant = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s
  • ν = Frequency (Hz)
  • c = Speed of light = 3×10¹⁰ cm/s
  • λ = Wavelength (cm)

Each substance has a unique absorption spectrum - a "fingerprint" that identifies it.
